Tools, frameworks and reading curated for sports coaches and PE staff.
Mental Fortitude
🧘
Box Breathing — 4-4-4-4
Inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4. Use before a high-pressure fixture or difficult conversation. Activates your parasympathetic nervous system and reduces cortisol.
2 min · Breathing
🎯
Process vs Outcome Focus
When results feel out of control, redirect your team to what they can control — preparation, attitude, effort, communication. Write down 3 process goals before every fixture.
Mindset · Coaching
💬
Reframing Self-Talk
Replace "I can't handle this" with "This is difficult and I am managing it." Replace "They're not listening" with "I need to find a different approach." Language shapes reality.
Daily practice
Empathy in Coaching
👂
The 3-Second Pause
Before responding to a learner who is upset, frustrated or disengaged — pause for 3 full seconds. This single habit reduces reactive responses by over 60% according to sports psychology research.
Instant · Relationship
🧩
Understanding the Iceberg
Behaviour is the tip. Below the surface: fear of failure, home pressures, peer dynamics, identity. When a learner acts out, ask — what am I not seeing? What might be happening beneath this?
Framework · Learners
🌡
Check the Temperature First
Start every practice with a 30-second "temperature check" — ask learners to show 1-5 fingers for how they are feeling. You will coach completely differently when you know a learner is at 1.
5 min · Team culture
Reflection Practice
📓
The 3-2-1 Post-Session Review
After every session: write 3 things that went well, 2 things to improve, 1 thing a learner did that surprised or inspired you. Takes 4 minutes. Compounds into significant growth over a season.
4 min · Post-session
🔄
Gibbs Reflective Cycle
Description → Feelings → Evaluation → Analysis → Conclusion → Action plan. A structured framework for deep professional reflection. Use monthly to review your coaching identity and direction.
Framework · Monthly
Wellbeing and Recovery
🌙
The Recovery Triangle
Sleep, nutrition and movement are the foundation of your coaching capacity. When you are depleted, your empathy, patience and decision-making all suffer before you notice. Protect all three.
Lifestyle · Foundation
🚧
Setting Healthy Limits
Coaches often carry emotional loads well beyond their role — parent complaints, learner welfare, admin pressure. It is not selfish to protect your time and energy. A depleted coach cannot develop others.
Self-care · Sustainability
